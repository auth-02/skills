// Field Notes · No. 01 · Interstellar

Two probes,
forty-eight years,
still talking.

Launched in 1977 to tour the outer planets, Voyager 1 and 2 kept going. They are now the most distant human-made objects in existence — small, cold, plutonium-warmed machines whispering back across light-hours of empty space.

// 02 — TrajectoryDISTANCE FROM THE SUN

Falling outward, forever.

Neither probe will ever return. With no atmosphere to slow them and no destination to reach, they coast outward at a steady clip — Voyager 1 a little faster, having taken the more aggressive slingshot past Saturn.

Voyager 1 Voyager 2
// 03 — The Grand TourA RARE ALIGNMENT

A planetary alignment that comes once in 176 years.

The mission existed because the outer planets happened to line up so a single craft could swing from one to the next, each gravity assist flinging it onward. Miss the window and you'd wait until the 2150s.

  • 1977Both launch. Voyager 2 leaves first, on Aug 20; Voyager 1 follows on Sep 5, on a faster track.
  • 1979Jupiter. Active volcanoes found on the moon Io — the first ever seen beyond Earth.
  • 1980–81Saturn. Voyager 1 dives past Titan, bending its path up and out of the planetary plane for good.
  • 1986Uranus. Voyager 2 alone, the only craft ever to visit.
  • 1989Neptune. The last planet. Voyager 2 completes the tour and turns toward deep space.
  • 1990The Pale Blue Dot. Voyager 1 turns back for a final family portrait of the solar system.
  • 2012Interstellar. Voyager 1 crosses the heliopause — the edge of the Sun's bubble.
  • 2018Voyager 2 follows across the heliopause, six years behind its twin.
// 04 — The CraftTWINS, NOT IDENTICAL

Less computing power than a key fob.

Each probe carries about 70 kilobytes of memory and is warmed by the slow decay of plutonium-238. Engineers now switch instruments off one at a time to stretch the dwindling power budget for a few more years.

// 05 — The Golden RecordA MESSAGE IN A BOTTLE

A gold-plated hello, for whoever finds it.

Bolted to each probe is a phonograph record of copper plated in gold, etched with instructions for playback. It is built to survive a billion years in the vacuum — a portrait of Earth assembled in case anyone, ever, is listening.

Format · 01
12-inch disc

Gold-plated copper, with a cartridge and stylus included. A diagram on the cover shows how to play it.

Greetings · 02
55 languages

Spoken hellos from Akkadian to Wu, plus a message from the UN Secretary-General.

Images · 03
115 photos

Encoded as audio: anatomy, landscapes, a supermarket, a nursing mother, the structure of DNA.

Music · 04
90 minutes

Bach and Chuck Berry, Javanese gamelan, a Navajo night chant, a Bulgarian shepherdess.

This is a present from a small, distant world, a token of our sounds, our science, our images, our music, our thoughts and our feelings. — Carter's message, etched aboard · 1977
